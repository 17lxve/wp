No it's not
Mangas are a form a litterarature that originate from Japan. They are used to convey stories, meaning and ideals to people, and are as varied in themes and styles as any other genre. The readers are a gigantic and creative community, that exchanges everyday and try to make the world a better place.

Most manga conform to a style developed in Japan in the late 19th century, and the form has a long prehistory in earlier Japanese art. The term manga is used in Japan to refer to both comics and cartooning. Outside of Japan, the word is typically used to refer to comics originally published in the country.

People of all ages read manga. The medium includes works in a broad range of genres: action, adventure, business and commerce, comedy, detective, drama, historical, horror, mystery, romance, science fiction and fantasy, erotica (hentai), sports and games, and suspense, among others.

The word "manga" comes from the Japanese word 漫画 composed of the two kanji 漫 (man) meaning "whimsical or impromptu" and 画 (ga) meaning "pictures". The same term is the root of the Korean word for comics, "manhwa", and the Chinese word "manhua". The word first came into common usage in the late 18th century with the publication of such works as Santō Kyōden's picturebook 'Shiji no yukikai' in 1798, and in the early 19th century with such works as Aikawa Minwa's Manga hyakujo in 1814 and the celebrated Hokusai Manga books (1814–1834) containing assorted drawings from the sketchbooks of the famous ukiyo-e artist Hokusai. Rakuten Kitazawa (1876–1955) first used the word "manga" in the modern sense.

Traditionally, manga stories flow from top to bottom and from right to left.
